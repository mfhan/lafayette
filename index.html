He sailed to America at age 19. Alongside Washington, Jefferson and Hamilton, he played a key role in establishing the United States.
Forty years later, in the summer of 1824, Lafayette visited the young nation he helped build and was met with an overwhelming outpouring of affection and gratitude.
New York City still bears witness to that historic journey.

Delirious Crowds and Sumptuous Balls

He made his first landing in New York City, where 50,000 people, or one-third of the population of the time, lined up to welcome him.

He went here and there -- blls and parades. Grown men waited in line to merely touch his hand. He met dignitaries and ordinary people.

When he came to Brooklyn to lay the cornerstone of the structure that would later become the Brooklyn Museum, he embraced a six-year-old boy who never forgot about the encounter. The boy's name was Walt Whitman.
